# Genetic Polymorphisms of Cytochrome P450 Enzymes and Transport Proteins in a Russian Population and Three Ethnic Groups of Dagestan.

## Metadata
**Authors:** Karin B Mirzaev, Dmitry A Sychev, Kristina A Ryzhikova, Olga D Konova, Suleiman N Mammaev, Daniyal M Gafurov, Grigorij N Shuev, Elena A Grishina, Zhannet A Sozaeva
**Journal:** Genetic testing and molecular biomarkers
**Year:** 2017
**PMID:** 29023140

## Abstract

**AIM:** The objective of this study was to investigate the prevalence of polymorphic markers of the CYP2C19, CYP2C9, CYP2D6, SLCO1B1, and ABCB1 genes among the three ethnic groups in Dagestan and compare it with the carrier frequency of these markers among the Russian population living in Moscow.

**METHODS:** The study involved 186 healthy, unrelated, and chronic medication-free volunteers (53 males and 133 females) of the three ethnic groups in the Dagestan Republic: 46 Laks, 90 Avars, and 50 Dargins. Genotyping was performed using real-time polymerase chain reaction-based methods. The allelic prevalences of the three Dagestan peoples were compared with ethnic Russians from the Moscow region.

**RESULTS:** Statistically significant differences for the following gene polymorphisms: CYP2C19*17, CYP2C9*3, ABCB1 (C3435T), SLCO1B1*5 were found between the Russian population and the three ethnic groups of the Dagestan republic.

**CONCLUSION:** The data obtained from this study will help with prioritization genotyping in the region.
