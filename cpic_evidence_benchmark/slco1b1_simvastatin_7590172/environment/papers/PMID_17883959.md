# Organic anion transporting polypeptide-1B1 haplotypes in Chinese patients.

## Metadata
**Authors:** Lin-Yong Xu, Yi-Jing He, Wei Zhang, Sheng Deng, Qing Li, Wei-Xia Zhang, Zhao-Qian Liu, Dan Wang, Yuan-Fei Huang, Hong-Hao Zhou, Zhen-Qiu Sun
**Journal:** Acta pharmacologica Sinica
**Year:** 2007
**PMID:** 17883959

## Abstract

**AIM:** To detect 388G>A and 521T>C variant alleles in the organic anion transporting polypeptide-1B1 (OATP1B1, encoding gene SLCO1B1) gene.

**METHODS:** One hundred and eleven healthy volunteers were screened for OATP1B1 alleles in our study. PCR-restriction fragment length polymorphism was used to identify the 388G>A polymorphism and a 1-step tetra-primer method was developed for the determination of 521T>C mutation.

**RESULTS:** The frequencies of the 388G>A and 521T>C variant alleles in the Chinese population were 73.4% and 14.0%, respectively. The frequencies of the SLCO1B1*1b and *15 haplotypes were 59.9% and 14.0%, respectively.

**CONCLUSION:** The SLCO1B1*1b and SLCO1B1*15 variants are relatively common in the Chinese population. Their frequencies are similar to that in the Japanese, but significantly different from that in Caucasians and blacks.
